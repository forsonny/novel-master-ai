Title: The Glass Orbit
Genre: Solarpunk mystery thriller
Themes: community resilience, climate justice, trust vs secrecy
Target length: 110k words
POV: Dual first-person (engineer Aiko, investigator Rafael)
Tone: Optimistic but tense
Timeline: 3 acts, present day, orbital habitat around Venus

Act I – Spark
- Introduce orbital city Helion and energy cooperative
- Aiko discovers energy fluctuations tied to sabotage
- Rafael arrives to audit, distrust between leads
- Stakes: power loss would freeze lower decks, harm seedlings

Act II – Fracture
- Parallel investigations in engineering deck and street-level markets
- Reveal eco-fascist cult smuggling black-market sunshields
- Aiko learns cult manipulates her sister; conflict of loyalty
- Rafael uncovers council corruption; forced to go rogue with Aiko

Act III – Confluence
- Execute dual heist: disable cult sunshields + leak corruption proofs
- Mass blackout threatens Helion garden domes
- Aiko must convince sister to help reroute energy
- Final reveal: sabotage hides data theft; choose truth over safety

Key characters
- Aiko Tanaka – solar engineer, methodical, protective of family, motif: origami sun charms
- Rafael Costa – auditor, former journalist, struggles with cynicism, motif: analog recorder
- Mina Tanaka – Aiko’s sister, conflicted cult insider
- Councillor Nyla Vos – antagonist, believes in authoritarian stability

World rules
- Energy captured via orbital mirrors; redistribution is political power
- Citizens trade growth credits for access to garden domes
- Communication delays between orbital strata cause rumor-driven tension
- Black-market sunshields can sterilize entire decks if misaligned

Research hooks
- Look up real solarpunk city planning concepts
- Investigate how orbital mechanics impacts day/night cycles
- Study cult deprogramming techniques for Mina’s arc

Output expectations
- 8 top-level tasks (acts + cross-cutting initiatives)
- Each task should mention POV focus, emotional stakes, and test strategy referencing continuity/word-count checks
