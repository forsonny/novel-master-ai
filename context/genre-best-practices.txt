# Genre Best Practices for Novel Writing

This reference guide outlines genre-specific conventions, expectations, and best practices to help Novel Master generate appropriate story structures and narrative elements.

## Fantasy

### Conventions
- **World-building:** Extensive world-building with magic systems, mythical creatures, or alternate realities
- **Pacing:** Slower opening to establish world, faster pacing in action sequences
- **Word Count:** 80,000 - 120,000 words (adult), 60,000 - 90,000 (YA)
- **Structure:** Often follows hero's journey or quest narrative

### Key Elements
- Magic system with clear rules and limitations
- Mythical creatures or races with defined characteristics
- Political systems or power structures
- Prophecies or ancient lore
- Good vs. evil conflicts (though modern fantasy often subverts this)

### POV Considerations
- Multiple POVs common in epic fantasy
- Single POV more common in urban fantasy
- Third person limited most common

### Research Needs
- Mythology and folklore
- Historical periods (for secondary world inspiration)
- Linguistics (for constructed languages)
- Cultural anthropology

## Science Fiction

### Conventions
- **World-building:** Technology, scientific concepts, future or alternate timelines
- **Pacing:** Fast-paced, action-oriented, but with room for scientific explanation
- **Word Count:** 80,000 - 120,000 words
- **Structure:** Often follows problem-solving or discovery narrative

### Key Elements
- Scientific or technological concepts (hard SF) or speculative elements (soft SF)
- Exploration of "what if" scenarios
- Social commentary through futuristic lens
- Technology as both solution and problem
- Alien species or AI with defined characteristics

### POV Considerations
- Multiple POVs common in space operas
- Single POV more common in near-future SF
- Third person limited or omniscient

### Research Needs
- Current scientific research
- Technology trends
- Space exploration
- AI and robotics
- Climate science

## Thriller

### Conventions
- **World-building:** Realistic contemporary or near-future settings
- **Pacing:** Fast-paced, high tension, short chapters
- **Word Count:** 70,000 - 100,000 words
- **Structure:** Linear timeline with escalating tension

### Key Elements
- High stakes and immediate danger
- Time pressure or ticking clock
- Plot twists and reveals
- Moral ambiguity
- Action sequences balanced with investigation

### POV Considerations
- Multiple POVs common (protagonist, antagonist, victims)
- First person or third person limited
- Short, punchy chapters

### Research Needs
- Police procedures
- Forensic science
- Technology (hacking, surveillance)
- Psychology (criminal behavior)
- Current events

## Literary Fiction

### Conventions
- **World-building:** Realistic, often contemporary settings
- **Pacing:** Slower, character-driven, contemplative
- **Word Count:** 60,000 - 100,000 words
- **Structure:** Less plot-driven, more character and theme-focused

### Key Elements
- Character development over plot
- Exploration of themes and human condition
- Beautiful prose and literary techniques
- Ambiguity and open endings
- Social commentary

### POV Considerations
- Single POV most common
- First person or third person limited
- Deep character interiority

### Research Needs
- Historical periods (if period piece)
- Cultural contexts
- Psychology and human behavior
- Literary techniques
- Social issues

## Romance

### Conventions
- **World-building:** Contemporary, historical, or fantasy settings
- **Pacing:** Moderate, with emotional beats
- **Word Count:** 50,000 - 100,000 words (varies by subgenre)
- **Structure:** Meet-cute, obstacles, resolution (HEA/HFN)

### Key Elements
- Central romantic relationship
- Emotional conflict and growth
- Happy ending (HEA) or happy for now (HFN)
- Chemistry and tension between leads
- External and internal obstacles

### POV Considerations
- Dual POV common (both love interests)
- First person or third person limited
- Alternating chapters between POVs

### Research Needs
- Historical periods (for historical romance)
- Cultural contexts
- Relationship psychology
- Setting details

## Mystery

### Conventions
- **World-building:** Realistic settings, often small communities
- **Pacing:** Moderate, with reveals at key points
- **Word Count:** 70,000 - 90,000 words
- **Structure:** Investigation, red herrings, reveal

### Key Elements
- Central mystery or crime
- Investigation process
- Clues and red herrings
- Fair play (reader can solve it)
- Resolution that explains everything

### POV Considerations
- Single POV (detective/investigator)
- First person or third person limited
- Occasional POV from other characters

### Research Needs
- Police procedures
- Forensic science
- Legal processes
- Psychology (criminal behavior, investigation techniques)

## Horror

### Conventions
- **World-building:** Realistic or supernatural settings
- **Pacing:** Slow build to intense climax, atmospheric tension
- **Word Count:** 70,000 - 100,000 words
- **Structure:** Escalating fear, reveal, confrontation

### Key Elements
- Atmosphere and mood
- Fear and dread
- Supernatural or psychological horror
- Isolation or vulnerability
- Unsettling reveals

### POV Considerations
- Single POV most common
- First person or third person limited
- Deep psychological interiority

### Research Needs
- Psychology of fear
- Historical events (for period horror)
- Supernatural beliefs
- Medical/forensic details

## Young Adult (YA)

### Conventions (across genres)
- **World-building:** Varies by genre
- **Pacing:** Fast-paced, action-oriented
- **Word Count:** 60,000 - 90,000 words
- **Structure:** Coming-of-age arc, often with genre elements

### Key Elements
- Teen protagonist (typically 16-18)
- Coming-of-age themes
- First love or relationships
- Identity exploration
- Agency and empowerment

### POV Considerations
- Single POV most common
- First person very popular
- Present tense common

### Research Needs
- Teen culture and language
- School systems
- Social media trends
- Issues affecting teens

## General Best Practices Across Genres

### Pacing Guidelines
- **Opening:** Hook within first page, inciting incident by chapter 3
- **Middle:** Escalate tension, raise stakes, develop characters
- **Ending:** Satisfying resolution that addresses main conflict

### Chapter Length
- **Thriller:** 2,000 - 3,000 words (short, punchy)
- **Literary:** 3,000 - 5,000 words (longer, contemplative)
- **Fantasy/SF:** 3,000 - 5,000 words (world-building space)
- **Romance:** 2,500 - 4,000 words (emotional beats)
- **Mystery:** 2,500 - 4,000 words (investigation pacing)

### Scene Structure
- **Goal:** What does the character want?
- **Conflict:** What stands in their way?
- **Disaster:** What goes wrong?
- **Reaction:** How do they respond?
- **Dilemma:** What choice do they face?
- **Decision:** What do they decide?

### Dialogue
- **Purpose:** Advance plot, reveal character, create conflict
- **Voice:** Distinct voices for each character
- **Subtext:** What's not being said
- **Pacing:** Vary dialogue with action and description

### Description
- **Sensory details:** Sight, sound, smell, touch, taste
- **Specificity:** Concrete details over abstract
- **Pacing:** Balance description with action
- **Purpose:** Every description should serve story or character

## Using Genre Conventions in Novel Master

When creating your NRD:
1. **Specify genre** in the Overview section
2. **Reference conventions** in your act structure
3. **Include genre-specific elements** in world rules
4. **Note pacing preferences** in revision priorities
5. **Use genre templates** as starting points

Novel Master's AI prompts are tuned to recognize genre conventions and generate appropriate narrative structures, pacing, and elements based on your NRD's genre specification.
